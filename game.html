The Game

Mechanics, systems, and world design from the current build.

Built from the ocean up

Subnautica 2 is an open-world survival game set on an alien ocean planet. You gather resources, craft tools, and build underwater habitats — all while something large and unknown watches from the deep.

The early access build includes three full biomes, a complete crafting tree, base-building, and the first chapter of the story. Multiplayer supports up to four players.

Core Systems

How the game works

Survival Loop

Track oxygen, hunger, water, and temperature at all times. The ocean environment shifts — cold currents drain heat, strong pressure damages your suit below certain depths. You adapt or you die.

Crafting & Tech Tree

Start with a scanner and a knife. Work up to seamoths, exosuits, and a full underwater command center. Over 120 items span five tiers, from basic tools to advanced quantum storage modules.

Base Building

Construct modular habitats on the seafloor or anchored to rock faces. Add grow beds, fabricators, vehicle bays, and observation domes. Structural integrity matters — poor placement floods your base.

Exploration

No map. No waypoints. You scan the environment, log creatures, and build your own knowledge base. The world is procedurally seeded but hand-crafted at the biome level — no two playthroughs start the same.

Multiplayer Co-op

Play with up to three friends in shared survival sessions. Resources, base components, and story progress sync across all players. Drop-in / drop-out works without pausing the world.

Creature Ecology

Marine life follows real behavioral systems. Predators hunt prey. Schools scatter under threat. Some creatures can be tamed or farmed. Aggression toward the player is earned, not scripted.

Current Build

Three biomes in Early Access

01

The Shallows

Warm, sunlit waters down to 200m. Kelp forests, coral, and abundant food. It looks safe. It mostly is — during the day.

  • Max depth: 200m
  • Temperature: 18–24°C
  • Threat level: Low
02

The Thermocline

A transition zone of unstable pressure and shifting currents between 200m and 600m. Navigation is disorienting. Resources are rich.

  • Max depth: 600m
  • Temperature: 4–10°C
  • Threat level: Medium
03

The Abyss Shelf

Below 600m, bioluminescent creatures replace sunlight. Pressure requires upgraded suits. Something down here is not biological.

  • Max depth: 1,800m
  • Temperature: 0–2°C
  • Threat level: Extreme
